# Hedging Concept

This is one of the MOST IMPORTANT concepts in finance.

### Step 1

Buy physical gold

1 kg Gold

### Step 2

Sell Gold Futures

1 kg Gold Futures

---

### Scenario A

Gold rises

Physical Gold Profit:

+₹10 lakh

Gold Futures Loss:

−₹10 lakh

Net:

0

---

### Scenario B

Gold falls

Physical Gold Loss:

−₹10 lakh

Gold Futures Profit:

+₹10 lakh

Net:

0

Result:

Price gets locked.

This is called:

## Hedging

Businesses use commodities mainly for hedging.

---

## Real World Example

Pizza companies need wheat.

If wheat price doubles:

Pizza cost increases.

So companies hedge wheat prices using commodity contracts.

Large businesses hedge:

* Gold
* Silver
* Crude Oil
* Natural Gas
* Wheat
* Cotton

# Futures

## Definition

An agreement today

for a transaction in the future

at a fixed price.

---

## Nifty Example

Suppose:

Nifty = 26,000

Buyer says:

"Nifty will rise."

Seller says:

"Nifty will fall."

Both enter a Futures Contract.

---

### Scenario A

Nifty → 27,000

Buyer Profit:

₹1000

Seller Loss:

₹1000

---

### Scenario B

Nifty → 25,000

Buyer Loss:

₹1000

Seller Profit:

₹1000

---

## Key Point

Futures Contract =

Mandatory obligation

Buyer and seller must honor the contract.

---

# Options

Options are different.

Buyer pays:

Premium

Like a booking amount.

---

## House Booking Analogy

Suppose:

House Price = ₹50 lakh

You pay:

₹50,000 token advance

If property price rises:

You buy.

If price falls:

You walk away.

Only token amount is lost.

This is exactly how options work conceptually.

---

## Nifty Example

Nifty = 26,000

Buyer says:

"If Nifty reaches 27,000, I may buy."

For that right:

He pays premium.

Suppose:

Premium = ₹100

If Nifty does not reach 27,000:

Maximum loss = ₹100

This limited-risk nature is why options are popular.
